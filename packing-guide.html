The Suitcase 🧳🧥

Accommodations WILL have a washing machine, so pack for 5-7 days and plan to do laundry once. You DO NOT need an outfit for every day. The hotel has a washer but no dryer (buy your own detergent); local laundromats have combo units with detergent. Use packing cubes or vacuum bags to maximize space for souvenirs.

Here is what to pack in terms of clothing for Late October–Early November in Tokyo:

  • Tops:
    • 2 Short-sleeve tees (good indoor base layers)
    • 2 Long-sleeve tops
    • 1–2 Sweaters/Cardigans
    • 1 Nicer top or dress (optional)
  • Bottoms:
    • 4 Everyday pants/bottoms (jeans, trousers, long skirts etc.)
    • Optional: 1 extra bottom if you dislike re-wearing pants between washes
    • Shorts are generally not needed for this time of year.
  • Outerwear:
    • 1 Light jacket
    • 1 Warmer layer (packable jacket, puffer vest, or hoodie)
    • 1 Scarf (optional)
    • You do NOT need a heavy winter coat.
  • Undergarments & Sleep:
    • ~7 Underwear
    • 7–9 Socks (bring extra in case of rain)
    • 2-4 Sets of sleep clothes
  • Shoes:
    • 1 Primary walking sneaker
    • Optional 2nd pair
    • Keep in mind you will be wearing one of these pairs to the airport.

Late October–early November in Tokyo is cool and comfortable during the day and noticeably chilly at night. Expect mild afternoons (low 60s–low 70s °F), cooler evenings (upper 40s–50s °F), occasional rain, and early sunsets. Tokyo is a walking/transit city, and autumn is one of the most comfortable seasons for long outdoor days. Some travelers report that it can feel warmer during the day, especially in direct sun or in train stations. Think LAYERS instead of bulky outfits. The key is making sure your layers are easy to take off and carry once you go indoors. 🍁👚

The Personal Item ✈️

This bag can fit in front of the seat in front of you on a plane and might be the only bag you can access on the plane.

Include in your Personal Item:

  • Spare change of clothes (if desired)
  • Chargers + cables/power bank (must stay in carry-on or personal item, cannot be checked)
  • Earbuds/Headphones
  • EMPTY water bottle (optional, fill up after TSA)
  • Medications (in original bottles + documentation if needed)
  • Wet wipes, snacks, chapstick
  • Electronics (tablet, switch, laptop, etc.)
  • Toiletry Bag - see below (pack at the top for easy access through TSA)

⭐Japan's medication import rules can be strict, and the safest approach is to verify your specific meds well before departure.


Include in your Toiletry Bag 🧴:

  • Toothbrush + Toothpaste
  • Deodorant
  • Sunscreen
  • Body Lotion, Make-up (optional)
  • Shampoo + Conditioner
  • Body Wash
  • Face Wash
  • Hand Sanitizer
  • Feminine products (optional)
  • Contact lens supplies (if needed)

Make sure all fluids are within TSA Guidelines.

⭐ Please note the hotel will provide shampoo, conditioner and bodywash. Unless you are picky about the products you use, you can always use the products made available to you at the hotel OR you could always purchase what you need while in Tokyo.

The Everyday Bag 🎒💳

An everyday bag will greatly help while going out and exploring. Most likely this will not be packed in your suitcase and will be carried with you to the airport. This can consist of a purse, fanny pack, or even a drawstring bag. If you chose a purse, try and find a small crossbody bag. Crossbodies and fanny packs will be considered part of your person, and not a personal item. A purse or a drawstring bag might need to go into your personal item until you are passed TSA. Here is what should be in your everyday bag for both on the plane and while walking around Japan.

Everyday Bag Essentials:

  • Wallet:
    • Cash
      • You will be given Japanese Yen to spend daily
      • Japan is mostly a cash based society still so this will be your main form of payment.
    • Debit card/credit card
      • Be sure to check if your cards work in Japan and don't have ridiculous transaction fees.
  • Passport
    • Keep this on your person at all times when not in our lodgings
  • Battery Bank and Charge Cable
  • Suica Card/JR pass (We get in Japan)
  • Travel Folder (Provided by the Team)

While in Japan, it might be a good idea to have a small bag to carry a small tote styled or collapsible bag to help carry some larger daily items such as your umbrella, sunscreen, water bottle and any souvenirs you might pick up.

Random Items 🌂🛍️

A few random items that you could buy while in Japan but you should consider packing as well:

  • Small travel umbrella (occasional rain expected)
  • Small-medium bag to carry with your everyday bag, for anything such as your umbrella, water bottle, sunscreen and souvenirs.
